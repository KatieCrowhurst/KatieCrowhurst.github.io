Who am I?

My name is Katie Crowhurst and I currently work as a Junior Developer for Integrated Care 24 (IC24).

My Journey

My journey to get to where I am today has most definitely been interesting. I actually come from a Child Psychology background – I studied a Childcare Diploma at college and went on to complete a Bachelor’s degree in Childhood and Youth Studies with Psychology. Unfortunately this was the first year that the university were running this course, and during my final year of study, we were told that they had not succeeded in getting accreditation from the British Psychological Society (BPS) for the course like they had hoped. I had hoped to pursue a career as an Educational Psychologist, but this news meant that the journey would be a lot tougher, and in the end, something that financially I would not be able to afford.

After moving back home with my parents and working in roles where I was unfortunately able to progress, I started heavily researching into new career sectors that I could venture into. I wanted a role that was challenging, exciting - something completely new where I could really succeed. My partner was a Network Engineer at the time, studying for Cisco certifications, and on weekends when I would visit him we would watch some of the course content together. This, and his support, encouraged me to delve into the IT sector, where I discovered I was extremely interested in web development.
It was new, exciting, challenging, with a huge variety of learning experiences and paths to follow, and actually very rewarding. I love the feeling of building something up from scratch!

My Learning

I taught myself HTML, CSS, JavaScript and the basics of MySQL over roughly a year and a half. In the early stages of my learning I created a basic Coding Journal website which gave me a clear understanding of HTML and CSS. I also started to create a basic “About Me” website, which has now been re-purposed in the building of this website.
Once I felt comfortable with HTML and CSS, I took a Udemy course to learn JavaScript. The course combined theory and practical projects, and really allowed me to become confident in using JavaScript. I was able to manipulate my HTML elements, create event handlers and provide interactivity in my pages.
